## Favorite courses

In no particular order, here's a list of a few classes I've taken at U of M that I really liked and would highly recommend.


### MATH 216

216 is the differential equations course in the standard calculus sequence. People are often too haunted by Michigan's calcs 1 and 2 to take the final two, which is understandable. I was lucky enough to place out of them and brought none of the associated trauma into the course.

From what I can tell, the class is a pretty standard diffeq course. Solving first order equations, then second order, then systems, Laplace transforms, etc. A few elements improved my experience, namely:

* My professor, Selim Esedoglu, who explained the content very well and created a motivating lecture environment.
* The course's computational labs. Every week, we had to attend a small group session in a computer lab and model some equation(s) related to the coursework in Matlab. Most people are not very fond of the labs because they expect you to know Matlab but never teach it, and so the period primarily entails copying and pasting code and debugging. Knowing Matlab, however, I was able to appreciate what they wanted us to learn and feel a lot more confident in solving differential equations computationally as a result.
* Stumbling onto 3Blue1Brown's [Essence of Linear Algebra](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fNk_zzaMoSs&list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab) series at the right point in the course. The class teaches the bare minimum amount of linear algebra to do systems, which is less than is necessary to understand what's actually going on under the good. 3Blue1Brown's series successfully provides a lot of the intuition behind the math, most importantly abstract vector spaces for the course, and let me see past the computational steps to the mathematical beauty.

### MCDB 310

Introductory biochemistry gets a bad wrap. Premeds complain about having to memorize a bunch of pathways and recite them exactly on the exam. This is an element of the class to some extent, but viewing the coursework more broadly provides a greater understanding and appreciation of the content.

The class spans from relevant chemistry to the classes of biomolecules to (yes...) a wide variety of pathways associated with metabolism and synthesis. It is highly informed by energetics and thermodynamics and large portion of the exams rely on students being able to connect the impact of separate pathways on overall system function. Biochemistry is also incredibly interesting! My professors, Dr. Balazovich and Jakob instilled a deep appreciation for the most minute details of the fundamentals of life.

I mildly regret not taking the chemistry department's version of the class, but I highly enjoyed 310 regardless.
