## Experience

As a student at the University of Michigan, I've been pretty lucky to have access to a pretty wide number of experiences both in and outside of the sciences.

### Zhang Lab

Through the [Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program](https://lsa.umich.edu/urop), I was placed in the [Zhang Lab](https://zhanglab.ccmb.med.umich.edu/) in DMCB. The Zhang Lab is focused on structural bioinformatics, in particular the prediction of protein structure from sequence data and design of novel proteins. During my time in the lab, I used [EvoDesign](https://zhanglab.ccmb.med.umich.edu/EvoDesign), [I-TASSER](https://zhanglab.ccmb.med.umich.edu/I-TASSER/) and [QUARK](https://zhanglab.ccmb.med.umich.edu/QUARK/) to develop a protein similar to the ID family of transciption factor binding inhibitors, which negatively regulate cellular differentiation. Although I left the lab before the long process of experimental verification, I am grateful to the lab members, in particular Xiaoqiang Huang for sparking my interest in bioinformatics.

Here's the [poster](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/10fF8abLmtp5WlRc0rbuXYdRxCaG6YaRMW8R-zj56N4E/edit?usp=sharing) I presented at the 2019 UROP Spring Symposium.

### Welch Lab

The summer after my freshman year, I began working in the Welch Lab through the [MCubed Scholars](https://lsa.umich.edu/urop/Prospective-Students/summer-programs/mcubed-scholars-program.html) program. During that summer, I grew adept in the R programming language, learned about single cell multi-omic analysis software packages, and added functionality to [LIGER](https://macoskolab.github.io/liger/), an integrative nonnegative matrix factorization-based single cell analysis tool. I also applied LIGER to the analysis of single cell RNA- and ATACseq collected from mouse bone marrow.

Here's the [poster](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1jeQc31mbBFYLlgkADKtPDz3DeLtkmyYiBTw9XIdKorY/edit?usp=sharing) I presented at the 2019 UROP Summer Symposium.
